THE MOO BAR

Seattle, WA

While the Moo Bar doesn’t have the most outstanding milk tea or boba, they have the best presentation by far. Their drinks come in a mason jar with an adorable drawing and pun on the front, ranging from corgis to Totoro, and I’m a sucker for Studio Ghibli! I’d recommend their Matcha Latte and Miss Peachy.

YOUNG TEA

Seattle, WA

Located in the heart of International District, Young Tea prides itself on the quality of the drinks. They use fresh milk while making their drinks, making them taste more refreshing and bringing out the true flavor of the tea. I love their Signature Milk Tea and Iron Buddha Milk Tea!

BOBA UP

Seattle, WA

The possibilities are endless at this self-serve boba spot in U-District! Any type of milk tea, fruit tea, slush, topping, even salted cream topping - if you can imagine it, Boba Up has it. And you can add as much as you want to your fill-up cup! Quality is average, but the experience is definitely worth a visit.

BOBA GUYS

San Francisco, CA

Boba Guys is my all-time favorite boba spot, always worth the long lines and slightly hefty prices for me. Their Matchata is a perfect amount of sweetness, and you can’t go wrong with their Muscat Oolong! The quality of their tea AND boba is unmatched!

PURPLE KOW

Berkeley, CA

A staple for UC Berkeley students (Go Bears!), Purple Kow stands in downtown Berkeley. They're famous for their Iced Milk Drinks that come in massive cups, a sweet and delicious milky drink that I've yet to find anywhere else. I love ordering their Iced Milk Drink with Boba and Matcha Pudding. YUM!

HAPPY LEMON

Berkeley, CA

I'm never disappointed by the quality of Happy Lemon's drinks. Their milk teas are the perfect amount of sweet, and their fruit teas wonderfully refreshing. This is the first place I tried a Salted Cheese drink, and I've been sold ever since! My go-to drink is their Black Tea with Salted Cheese.

VIVI BUBBLE TEA

New York City, NY

If you're looking for an instagrammable boba shop, Vivi Bubble Tea is the place to go! Their bubble tea floats come with a cloud of cotton candy on top, along with some aesthetically pleasing sides. Quality is neither astounding nor disappointing, and you can decide for yourself whether it's worth the hype.

GONG CHA

New York City, NY

Gong Cha is a huge chain that you can find all over the world, and it never disappoints. The quality of their tea and boba is SO good! But their drinks do tend to lean on the sweeter side, so I would recommend going one sweetness level below your usual. I keep it simple here with a Pearl Milk Tea, 30% sweetness, 50% ice.

BAR PA TEA

New York City, NY

How unique! A fun twist on regular boba, Bar Pa Tea offers a milk tea soft serve with boba as a topping. The boba is chewy and delicious, and the ice cream is refreshing on a hot New York summer day. 10/10 would recommend!
